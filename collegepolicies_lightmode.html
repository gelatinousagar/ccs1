COLLEGE POLICIES ON ADMISSION & RETENTION

   

    
               I. Requirements for Admission
                        For Senior High School Graduates
                    • The College is open to all SHS graduates regardless of their tracks. Non-STEAM/STEAM and non-STEM/STEM SHS graduates, with any 
                    Math or Science subject below 85% average grade, are required to take the additional non-credit course Problem Solving and Logic 
                    Formulation (PSLF 00) prior to Introduction to Computing (CCS 1) and Fundamentals of Programming (CCS 2) professional course.

                    • Non-STEAM/STEAM graduates who will enroll in BSCS will have to take additional non-credit course as prerequisites to some 
                    professional courses (Math 00).

                    • Applicants will be accepted as regular first year students provided that the General Average is at least 80% (with no grades 
                    below 80% in any subject) and has taken the augmentation course PSLF 00 if there is a need to.

                    • Those applicants with general Average of at least 80% but have a grade below 80% in any subject may be accepted On Probation 
                    (OP) student. Policy for OP students applies.




                        For Shiftees
                    • Those shifting from other Schools/Colleges of the University may be allowed to shift to any degrees offered by the College 
                     provided that the student has a Cumulative Quality Point Average (CQPA) of at least 2.0. Those who fall below the 2.0 cut-off 
                     will be accepted as On Probation students.
                     
                    • Those without programming courses in their previous School/Colleges of the University will be required to take the bridging 
                     course Problem Solving and Logic Formulation (PSLF 00)



                        For Transferees
                  Applicants from other schools and universities may be admitted to the College provided 
                  that the student:
                  
                  1. Has a QPA of not less than 2.0 or its equivalent. Those who fall below the 2.0 cut-off will be accepted with an On Probation status.

                  2. Submits a Transcript of Records (TOR) and complete description of computer courses taken (course outline and syllabus) in other 
                  schools for proper evaluation and validation.

                  3. Repeats all major courses if the student comes from a non-COE/COD in ITE, non-PAASCU, non-ACSCU, or non-PICAB accredited 
                  school/university.

                  4. takes the bridging course Problem Solving and Logic Formulation (PSLF 00) if the previous enrolled degree from other 
                  schools and universities doesn’t have programming courses.



                        Note:
                 • Validation exams may be given to transferees/shiftees, if deemed necessary by the Department Chair.      

                 • Only first year and second year professional courses and common professional courses found in any CCS curriculum will be 
                 considered for advance credits.

                 • Students coming from schools or universities outside the Philippines have to be evaluated by the respective Department 
                 Chairpersons for advanced credits.



                       For Second Coursers
                 Non-K to 12  second coursers will take all General Education courses



                       For Foreign Students
                A committee composed of the Department Chairpersons and/or faculty will decide on the admission of foreign students. 
                A signature indicating their approval will be required by the University Office of Registrar and Admissions prior to enrollment.



               II. Requirements for Retention
                        a. Minimum Grade Requirement
                The minimum grade requirement for a student to be able to proceed to the next professional course and/or semester for BSCS, 
                BSIT, and BSIS is 1.0 while for BLIS is 2.0.



                        b. Cumulative Quality Point Average
                The student needs to maintain a CQPA of 2.0 to remain in the program and to graduate and earn the degree. CQPA is to be 
                calculated every semester.



               III. Other related matters
                       a. On Probation 

                If the student's CQPA falls below 2.0, he/she will be placed in the "On Probation" status watch list. The student can 
                only be in OP status for two (2) consecutive semesters. After the two (2) consecutive semesters, the student will 
                be advised to shift to another degree.

                       b. Cumulative Quality Point Average

                If the student's CQPA falls below 2.0, the student is required to enroll remedial courses to improve his/her CQPA. 
                Upon evaluation and recommendation from the Department Chairperson and approval of the Dean, the student is required 
                to take in the current semester remedial course/s to increase the CQPA. The total number of units must not to exceed 
                the allowable maximum load in that semester. Remedial courses can be new courses, or the course/s with the lowest grade.

                       c. Removal Exam

                Faculty will not give removal exams.

               IV. Requirements for Shifting within CCS
                Automatic shifting is not allowed. A CCS student who wants to shift to another degree offered by the College must get 
                an approval from the Department Chairperson of the program where she/he wants to shift. 

                V. Policy on Cross-Enrollment
                • Any student from other units of the University who cross-enrolls in any of the professional or elective course of the 
                College must satisfy all the prerequisite requirements of the course.

                • CCS students are not allowed to take professional or elective courses outside the College/University.

                VI. Policy on Crediting Non-Professional Courses
                • Only the Dean and any of the Department Chairpersons can sign the Advanced Credit Form.
                
                • Students coming from non-accredited schools/universities (COE/COD in ITE, PAASCU, ACSCU, PICAB) need to retake the computer course. 

                • Students coming from COE/COD in ITE, PAASCU, ACSCU, or PICAB accredited schools/universities shall present a copy of their 
                transcript of records (TOR) and a description of the course (or courses) taken at their former schools.
    

    




CCS GUIDELINES


               I. ON EXCUSED ABSENCES
                     An absence is only considered excused when supported by valid documents. Below are the possible reasons and the corresponding 
                guidelines to be satisfied before an absence can be considered valid.
            
                       1. When a student gets sick, the following should be presented:
                           
                        • An excuse letter duly signed by the parents or guardian; 
                        • A medical certificate issued by the University doctor to support the excuse.

                     Medical certificates not coming from the University doctor is unacceptable, except those coming from specialists. 
                (e.g. for eye problems, the certificate must come from an EENT specialist)

                       2. If the illness is minor and a medical certificate is not possible, the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator is 
                       given the task to determine the validity of the absence.

                       3. When a student leaves for home to attend some personal/ important matters, the student must secure an excuse 
                       letter signed by the parents/guardian and the Guidance Counselor stating the reason(s);

                            Note:  Documents referred to in #1-#3 of this section must be presented to the
                        faculty concerned signed by the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator within the week the student returns to school, otherwise the 
                        absence automatically becomes unexcused.

                       4. When a student is sent to represent the University, he/she should:
                        
                        • secure in advance the excuse letter signed by the person in charge of the said event;
                        • present the excuse letter signed by the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator to the faculty concerned before leaving the University.
                       
                       5. When a student is required to appear before a public or private office, the student must:

                        • secure an excuse letter signed by the parents/guardian and the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator stating the reason;
                        • submit supporting document/s from the office that requires his/her presence;
                        • present the excuse letter to the faculty concerned before his/her scheduled appearance in the said office
                    
                       6. Students who may have mental or physical health issues must secure a psychiatric or health report from their 
                       psychiatrist or medical doctor. Those who have special cases/issues/ concerns that are highly confidential must secure 
                       a note from the Guidance Counselor.


               II. ON MAKE-UP EXAMINATIONS
                     1. It is the student's responsibility to take the initiative in approaching the faculty in taking make-up examinations.  

                     2. A student will only be allowed to take make-up examinations for major exams (e.g. prelim, midterm and final) provided 
                     that the absence is excused (see section on excused absences for guidelines).

                     3. Make-up exams for quizzes, long examinations, hands-on exams and hands-on exercises are faculty's prerogative. 

                     4. If the date of the examination is the same as the date a student is advised by a physician to return to class, the student 
                     may opt to postpone taking the exam.
                     
                     5. It is the faculty's discretion to set the make-up examination schedule.
                     
                     
               III. ON ATTENDANCE
                     1. Three (3) unexcused late arrivals (15 minutes after the scheduled time a course begins) is equivalent to 1 unexcused absence.

                     2. Three (3) consecutive unexcused absences will mean automatic dropping of the student from the class and a grade of "F".

                     3. Only three (3) unexcused and seven (7) excused absences are allowed for the entire semester. Penalty is the same as in #2 
                     of this section.
                    
                     
               IV. ON OTHER COMMON MATTERS


                     1. A student who has a grade of INC on any course has to complete the requirements of the same within one semester after the 
                     semester the course was taken, otherwise the grade will automatically become “NG”.
                     
                     2. Any student caught cheating in a major examination (prelim, midterm and finals) will automatically be given a grade of “F” 
                     on the course. For other exams / quizzes, the student will automatically be given a grade of “F” in that particular exam / quiz.
                     
                     3. All students must take major examinations as set by the College.
                     
                     4. Regarding prerequisite courses: If and when a student, due to an exceptional situation, has to take a prerequisite course 
                     simultaneously with a course requiring such a prerequisite, a failure in the prerequisite would mean no credit also for the 
                     course which required it, even if he/she passes the latter.
                     
                     5. Students of the College of Computer Studies (CSS) are required to attend all regular religious and academic convocations 
                     and assemblies. This policy also applies to all other special convocations/lectures/activities sponsored by the College and 
                     the University which are considered vital to the total development of the students. Students who are absent from the foregoing 
                     activities will be coursed for sanction/penalties as may be decided by the College and/or Confederates Student Council. 
                     College Activities may include but are not limited to: First Year Class Orientation, CCS Freshmen Orientation, All University 
                     Convocation, University Christian Life Emphasis Month (UCLEM), Orientation for Academically Challenged Students, 
                     CCS Founder’s Day, Juniors Team Building, Juniors BESS, College Days, Job Fair, Seniors Day, among others.

                     6. Overload is only allowed during the last semester period of schooling.
                     
                     
                V. ON COLLEGE CLEARANCE

                        The College clearance is a requirement before a student gets his/her proposed list (a list   of courses to be taken for the 
                    incoming semester). This clearance needs to be signed by the Confederates (CCS Students Council), Guidance Counselor, 
                    IRC Librarian, Department Chairperson and the Dean.

                    
                VI. ON GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS 

                       a. On Thesis/Capstone Project Requirement. 
                        No student is allowed to enroll in this course if he/she is not a candidate for graduation in March or summer of the 
                        current school year. A student will not be allowed to graduate if he/she fails to completely submit all requirements 
                        (hardbound of the manuscript and softcopy of the program).

                       b. On OJT/Internship/Practicum 
                        No student is allowed to enroll in this course if he/she is not a candidate for graduation in March or summer of the 
                        current school year. Any CSS student will not be allowed to graduate if he/she fails to comply the required number of 
                        OJT/internship/Practicum hours.



CCS AWARDS GUIDELINES


                RATIONALE:

                       The rationale of the CCS Awards is to recognize students who best epitomize Silliman University’s quality Christian 
                       education of academic excellence and student leadership.


                DEAN’S LIST AWARD: 

                       This award is given to regular students, regardless of year level, who have achieved and maintained a CQPA of at 
                       least 3.25.


                HIGHEST QPA AWARD:

                       This award is given to regular students, regardless of year level, who have achieved and maintained a CQPA of at 
                       least 3.25.


STUDENT/S OF THE YEAR AWARD

This award is given to students who have shown exceptional leadership abilities, excellence in academic endeavors, 
notable talents and unselfish services in line with the college’s goals. 

Scholarship would be based on a CQPA of at least 3.25, with no grades of F, WF, or INC. Activities credited will be based on the current 
school year only.

The Student/s of the Year Award are divided into four categories: Freshmen Student/s of the Year, Sophomore Student/s of the 
Year, Junior Student/s of the Year and Senior Student/s of the Year.





                                Criteria:

	                                    Service		25%
	                                    Leadership		25%
	                                    Scholarship		25%
	                                    Talent		25%
	                                    TOTAL		100%



Freshman Student/s of the Year
The student must meet the residency requirement of at least one semester of the entire freshmen year.

Sophomore Student/s of the Year
The student must meet the residency requirement of a full sophomore year and at least one semester.

Junior Student/s of the Year
The student must meet the residency requirement of a full junior year and at least 2 semesters.

Senior Student/s of the Year
The student meet the residency requirement of at least two years including the senior year.

TALENT AWARDS
These awards will be given to students with outstanding talents, outside of academic discipline, and who have 
generously shared them in enriching campus/College and/or community life.



                 The Talent Awards are categorized into the following distinctions:

                    • Excellence in Literary Arts
                    • Excellence in Performing Arts
                    • Excellence in Visual Awards
                    • Excellence in Athletics

                    Criteria:

                    Talent				50%
                    Scholarship			        25%
                    Leadership & Service		25%
                    TOTAL				100%

                         A residency of at least one year or two semesters including the current school year is required. Nominees are 
                   subject for a panel interview, if deemed necessary by the Committee. Scholarship should be based on the CQPA 
                   of at least 2.50 or better, with no grades of “F”, “WF” or “INC”. Activities should be based upon the past school year.


LEADERSHIP AND SERVICE AWARDS
These awards will be given to students of any year level as selected by the CCS Confederates Student Council adviser.

LABORATORY ASSISTANT OF THE YEAR AWARD
This award will be given to the most deserving CCS laboratory assistant (student assistant) as unanimously selected by the CCS Laboratory 
Assistants Adviser and the faculty.

SPECIAL AWARD
The Special Award is given to a student, who has been nominated by a faculty member, who shows exemplary performance in any of the above categories but cannot 
qualify due to certain grade requirements. The student must not be on On-Probation status.



 
        Selection process:

        1. Each department chairperson shall elect a member from his/her department to be a member of the committee.
        2. Faculty members will nominate students per category for the Leadership Award, and Laboratory Assistant Award.

The nominated students will be evaluated by a committee composed of an appointed Chairperson and appointed 
representatives from the 3 Departments and the Guidance Counselor/Facilitator. Only the representatives from the 3 Departments will be able to vote in any 
situation that requires a vote.
    

LABORATORY RULES
Students are expected to follow the following regulations for the use of the CCS Computer Laboratories and its facilities:


         1. Wear your SU official ID at all times.

         2. Only students who are enrolled in any of the computer courses are allowed to enter the lecture/laboratory rooms while classes are going on.

         3. The College facilities are for instructional purposes only. Students are not allowed to play with them.

         4. Students are cautioned against making loud noise/conversation which may disrupt classes in adjacent lecture rooms/laboratories.

         5. Bringing of food inside the lecture/laboratory rooms is strictly prohibited except during Thesis and Capstone Project defenses.

         6. Electronic devices (digital dictionary, smartphones, PDA'S MP3/MP4 players, etc.) can be used only upon the approval of the teacher.

         7. Using the College facilities to develop/send lewd, offensive, and threatening materials is cause for disciplinary action.

         8. If found violating any of the laboratory rules, the Dean or any of his duly authorized representatives has the option to suspend 

         and prohibit any student from the premises and may recommend disciplinary action to the university authorities.
    


INFORMATION RESOURCE CENTER (IRC)
Rules and Guidelines



         1. The IRC is open from 8:00 AM - 12 NN and 2:00 PM-5:00 PM from Monday to Friday or as scheduled by the Office of the Dean. Considering 
         the capacity of the IRC, a first come first serve basis will be observed.

         2. Only bonafide students, faculty and staff of CCS can borrow IRC materials for 2 days. A validated identification will be presented at the 
         Library Help Desk upon borrowing.

         3. Noise levels should be kept to a minimum. This includes the use of cell phones and music devices. Patrons are requested to turn 
         cellphone ringers to the lowest volume or to vibrate and take phone calls in the lobby area to minimize distraction.

         4. Food and beverages are allowed inside the IRC. Drinks should have a lid to help contain liquids in case of a spill. Patrons are responsible 
         for cleaning up and maintaining a clean environment. Aromatic foods that may bother other library 
         customers are discouraged in the IRC.

         5. Before a student leaves the library he/she is expected to leave the area clean, push in used chairs and return used book/s to the proper shelf/shelves.

EXAMINATION
All students are expected to take the preliminary, midterm and final examinations at the time scheduled by the office of 
the Dean of the concerned Department. If the student fails to take these exams for no justifiable reason, he/she is fined P 60 for each 
examination missed. The Dean shall determine if the reason is valid.



CHEATING
Per faculty action, June 3, 1987:
Any student caught cheating in a major exam (prelim, mid-term, and final) will be given an "F' for the 
course; and for other exams (chapter, quizzes), an "F" for such particular exam or quiz.



CLASS ATTENDANCE
Students are expected to attend all classes. Three (3) unexcused absences may be incurred. The rest of the 20% allowed by CHED must be excused, 
and incurred only for valid reasons.



STUDENT'S RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES



    1. Students and student organizations enjoy the right to freely examine and discuss all issues of interest and to express opinions privately or publicly. 
    
    2. It is the responsibility of all students to observe all duly approved University policies, rules and regulations, and those of the college and their 
    respective Departments. Violations are a cause for sanctions.

    3. The university shall handle disciplinary cases in a fair and most expeditious manner possible.

    4. The authority to impose disciplinary measures involving suspension, dismissal, and expulsion upon the recommendation of the University 
    Disciplinary Board (UDB) lies on the University President.


Students subjected to disciplinary hearings have the following rights:



        1. the right to notice of the time, date and place of hearing; 
    
        2. the right to present witnesses;

        3. the right to present evidence;

        4. the right to have an adviser; 

        5. the right to know the names and titles of the members of the University Disciplinary Board (UDB);

        6. the right to know the names of the complainant and other respondents, if any, and witnesses.





ACADEMIC VIOLATIONS



        1. Cheating, which may take, but are not limited to, the following forms:
            a. Looking at another student's answers during examinations;
            b. Showing or disclosing answers during the examination period;
            c. Using notes, notebooks, or textbooks, audio of visual recordings, or other 
            materials which are not explicitly authorized by the professor for student use during examinations;

        2. Plagiarizing is the act of taking ideas, writing from the works of another and passing them off as one's own, 
        or using the work of another without giving due credit.

        3. Violations of academic conduct also relate to stolen tests, falsified records, vandalism of library materials, 
        and breach  of computer security.

        4. Other forms of intellectual dishonesty such as knowingly furnishing false information, or omitting data that would provide the necessary 
        and required information to the Registrar, the instructor/teacher, University and College offices for any academically related purposes. 



VIOLATIONS OF UNIVERSITY POLICIES AND REGULATIONS


Disciplinary sanctions are imposed for violations of University policies and  regulations committed by the 
students or student groups within and outside the campus, adversely affecting the reputation of the University or the status of the students 
in school, which include, but are not limited to the following:
                         Disciplinary sanctions are imposed for violations of University policies and  regulations committed by the 

        1. Intoxication within the University.

        2. Theft of University or personal property.

        3. Vandalism on University or personal property

        4. Possession or use of firearms or deadly weapons of any kind or description

        5. Unauthorized possession of fireworks or other highly combustible materials

        6. Misuse of or tampering with the University’s electrical system, telephone service, fire safety equipment, and other security devices.

        7. Unauthorized entry into any University building, office or private property within the campus.

        8. Assault, physical or verbal abuse, intimidation, threats, harassment, coercion, or other conduct that that threaten or endanger the health 
        or safety of any person

        9. Malicious dissemination of false reports, threats of fire, explosion or other emergency.

        10. Unjustifiable or deliberate interference or disruption of academic functions of University activities which tend to create 
        disorder, breach of peace, or serious disturbance.

            The following are considered offenses/violations under this provision:
                a. Disruption of regular classes or operations inside the classrooms or offices without the permission of those concerned
                b. Disruption of co- and extra-curricular activities which include convocations, symposia, fora, and cultural presentations or shows
                c. Illegal demonstration o really accompanied by a public display of sentiment using force or violence and/or coercion 

        11. Forgery, falsification, unauthorized alteration or use of University documents or identification papers

        12. Hazing. The consent of those hazed shall not be considered a defense.

        13. Acts of lewdness, any act of immorality, sexual aggression or harassment;  possession, display or distribution of 
        pornographic materials within the University.

        14. The sale, use, or possession of prohibited substances such as shabu, marijuana, and other illegal or regulated drugs or drug 
        paraphernalia in violation of law or ordinance.

        15. The possession and drinking of liquor and alcoholic beverages inside the campus.

        16. Any other act committed by students or student groups within or outside the campus, which in the judgment of the University, 
        adversely affect the reputation of the University or the status of the students in the school.
